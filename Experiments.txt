Experiment 3: Dynamic Entry and Leaving

Meeting ID: demo-room

--------------------------------------------------------------
Event            | Device | Detected/Removed (Y/N) | Time | Notes
--------------------------------------------------------------
B joins later    | B202   | Y                      | ~2s  | B detects A immediately
B joins later    | A101   | N                      | -    | A did not detect B in this run
B stops sending  | B202   | Y                      | ~10s | Removed after timeout
--------------------------------------------------------------

Observations:
- When Device B joined later, it was able to detect Device A reliably.
- Device A did not consistently detect Device B after it joined.
- Device 2 (B202) had lower speaker volume compared to Device 1 (A101), which likely affected detection.
- Detection appears to be asymmetric depending on device hardware and signal strength.

What this proves:
- The system supports dynamic entry: newly joining devices can detect existing participants.
- The system supports dynamic leaving: devices are removed after they stop broadcasting.
- Detection reliability is affected by device capability (e.g., speaker loudness) and signal timing.
